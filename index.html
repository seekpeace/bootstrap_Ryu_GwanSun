Ryu Gawnsun

The Woman Who Lead A Nonviolent Protest Against Oppression Before Ghandi and Dr. Martin Luther King

also known as
YU GWAN SUN

  • In March 1919, the Korean Declaration of Independence was declared despite occupation.

  • She continued the March movement and was an organizer for peaceful demonstrations despite brutal crackdowns from the Japanese

  • March 1st Movement lead to the Korean Provisional Government and national unity

  • She was defiant against the Japanese regime despite tortue and died in prison

  • She never saw the independence of Korea but the movement lasted one month with 2 million people participating

  • Despite the protest failure, it showcased to the world a strong will for independence by the Korean people.

Japanese Colonial rule

  • 1910: Japan effectively annexed Korea
  • 1916: The final wave of Uibyeong rebels is defeated by Japanese forces.
  • 1919: March 1st Movement. Spurred by the sudden and mysterious death of Gojong. Declaration of Korean Independence. Nationceful demonstrations are crushed by Japanese military and police forces after two months. Governor-General Hasegawa resigns.
  • 1919: The establishment of The Provisional Government of the Republic of Korea in Shanghai.
  • 1919: Saito Makoto appointed as third Governor-General of Korea. The period of "cultural policy" begins.
  • 1920: Battle of Cheongsanri, Korean independence Army, led by Kim Jwa-jin, victory.
  • 1926: June 10th Movement.
  • 1932: Korean independence activist Lee Bong Chang fails in his attempt to assassinate Emperor Hirohito i
  • 1932: Korean independence activist Yun Bong Gil bombs Japanese Military gathering in Shanghai.
  • 1938: Governor-General of Korea begins Soshi-kamei (Order to Japanese-style name changes) policy.
  • 1945: The Empire of Japan surrenders to the Allies. According to the terms of Potsdam Declaration, Korea becomes independent.
